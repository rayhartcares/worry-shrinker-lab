For Parents & Teachers

Understanding what your child worked through today.

What this activity does

The Worry Shrinker Lab guides children ages 6–12 through a structured emotional processing sequence: naming the feeling, rating its intensity, examining it with gentle questions, applying a coping tool, and reflecting on what changed. This mirrors Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) techniques adapted for children.

How to follow up

  • Ask open questions: "What was one thing you learned about your worry today?"
  • Validate the feeling before redirecting: "That sounds really hard. I'm glad you named it."
  • Notice if the same worry appears repeatedly — that may be worth exploring with a counselor.
  • Celebrate the act of engaging with the worry, not just "feeling better."

When to seek more support

If a child consistently rates their worry at 8–10, describes physical symptoms (stomachaches, not sleeping), or avoids activities due to worry, it may be helpful to consult a school counselor or pediatric mental health professional.
