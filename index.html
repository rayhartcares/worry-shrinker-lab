What Is a Worry?

A worry is a thought that keeps coming back — usually about something that might happen or something that feels scary or hard.

🌧️

Worries can feel really big inside your mind, even when they're about something small.

💓

Worries can also show up in your body — like a tight tummy or a fast heartbeat.

⭐

Here's the good news: you can learn to understand your worries — and shrink them!

🔍

Shrink the Worry Tools

These tools can help you feel calmer when a worry feels big. Try each one!

🌬️

Talk to a Safe Grown-Up

Sharing a worry makes it smaller.

You can say: "I've been feeling worried about something. Can we talk?"

A safe grown-up is someone who listens and helps — like a parent, teacher, or counselor.

💡

Helpful Thought

Swap a big worry thought for a calmer one.

😟 "Something bad will definitely happen."
↓
😊 "I don't know for sure. I can handle it either way."
👣

One Small Brave Step

Do one tiny thing — even if it feels scary.

Think of one tiny step. Something small is still brave!
